What the numbers tell you

Switzerland pays significantly more: average monthly salary is $7,200/month vs $3,900/month in Netherlands — a gap of 1.8×. Housing costs follow the same direction: a studio in regional Switzerland averages $1,500/month vs $1,200/month in Netherlands — Netherlands is the cheaper base for renters.

Tax burden is where many expats are surprised. Switzerland sits at ~18% income tax at the mid-bracket vs ~28% in Netherlands — a gap that compounds quickly for freelancers, remote workers and small business owners.

Switzerland is for high-earning professionals who value political neutrality, safety, natural beauty and proximity to major European markets — and can absorb the high cost of living. Netherlands is for internationally mobile professionals, English speakers and those who value cycling culture, tolerance and a highly connected European base.
