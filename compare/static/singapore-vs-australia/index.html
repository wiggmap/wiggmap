What the numbers tell you

Average salaries are relatively close: Singapore at $5,500/month, Australia at $5,500/month — so the real differentiator is the cost side of the equation. Housing costs follow the same direction: a studio in regional Singapore averages $2,200/month vs $1,400/month in Australia — Australia is the cheaper base for renters.

Singapore offers maximum financial efficiency in a compact, ultra-modern city — no capital gains tax, zero income tax on foreign earnings, and world-class connectivity. Australia offers scale, natural environment and a clearer quality-of-life balance — with solid social services and more space, at the cost of higher taxes.

Australia is for those seeking an English-speaking, high-quality-of-life destination with strong institutions, outdoor culture and significant career opportunities. Singapore is for high-income professionals seeking Asia's most efficient city-state: zero crime, world-class infrastructure and no capital gains tax — at the cost of high living expenses.
