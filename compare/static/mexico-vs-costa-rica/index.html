What the numbers tell you

Average salaries are relatively close: Mexico at $900/month, Costa Rica at $850/month — so the real differentiator is the cost side of the equation. Housing costs follow the same direction: a studio in regional Mexico averages $450/month vs $450/month in Costa Rica — Costa Rica is the cheaper base for renters.

Safety is a meaningful differentiator here. Costa Rica scores 45 / 100 on the crime index vs 55 / 100 for Mexico — a gap that shapes daily life, insurance costs and the mental load of living abroad.

Costa Rica is for nature lovers, retirees and remote workers who prioritise safety, biodiversity and a stable democracy in a region known for instability. Mexico is for digital nomads, retirees and sun-seekers drawn to rich culture, proximity to the US, accessible residency and highly affordable living in cities like Oaxaca and Mérida.
