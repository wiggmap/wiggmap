What the numbers tell you

Portugal pays significantly more: average monthly salary is $1,400/month vs $600/month in Georgia — a gap of 2.3×. Housing costs follow the same direction: a studio in regional Georgia averages $370/month vs $570/month in Portugal — Georgia is the cheaper base for renters.

Beyond the numbers, the two countries offer fundamentally different propositions. Georgia's flat 1% tax for small businesses and zero tax on foreign income make it a magnet for digital nomads and crypto holders — Tbilisi has one of Europe's fastest-growing expat scenes. Portugal offers a more established path: EU membership, universal healthcare, and citizenship after 5 years of residence.

Portugal is for those who want European stability: retirees on the D7 visa, digital nomads seeking an EU legal framework, and families prioritising safety and long-term roots. Georgia is for digital nomads, crypto holders and budget-conscious expats drawn to low taxes, affordable living and a surprisingly vibrant Tbilisi scene.
